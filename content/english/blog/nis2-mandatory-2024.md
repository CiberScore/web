## What is NIS2 and why is it important?

The **NIS2 Directive** (Network and Information Systems Directive 2) came into force on January 16, 2023 and **is mandatory for all companies in essential and important sectors since October 2024**.

This new European regulation significantly expands the scope of the original NIS directive, including more sectors and establishing stricter cybersecurity requirements.

## Sectors affected by NIS2

### Essential sectors:
- **Energy** (electricity, oil, gas)
- **Transport** (air, rail, maritime, road)
- **Banking services**
- **Financial market infrastructures**
- **Healthcare**
- **Drinking water**
- **Wastewater**
- **Digital infrastructure**
- **Public administration**
- **Space**

### Important sectors:
- **Postal and courier services**
- **Waste management**
- **Chemical manufacturing**
- **Food production**
- **Manufacturing** (medical equipment, computers, etc.)
- **Digital service providers**
- **Research**

## Key compliance requirements

### Technical and organizational measures:
- **Risk management** in cybersecurity
- **Security measures** for systems and networks
- **Incident management** and business continuity plans
- **Supply chain management**
- **Encryption and communications security**

### Corporate governance:
- **Senior management** must approve cybersecurity measures
- **Regular supervision** of compliance
- **Cybersecurity training** for employees

## Non-compliance sanctions

NIS2 sanctions are significantly more severe:

- **Essential sectors**: Up to **10 million euros** or 2% of annual turnover
- **Important sectors**: Up to **7 million euros** or 1.4% of annual turnover
